# Act-based agents

- same thinking from Paul: there’s a “right” behaviour and a crater around it that has “good enough” behaviour, and we just want to land in the crater.
- these models only have as much foresight as the user themselves, since they model the user’s approval
    - if the user doesn’t approve of something they don’t know about, the model doesn’t take that kind of action
    - in a sense, the model is robust because it’s not smarter than the human in its strategising
