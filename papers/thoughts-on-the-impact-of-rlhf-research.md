# Thoughts on the Impact of RLHF Research

## Summary

Christiano reflects on whether RLHF research — which he pioneered at OpenAI — has been net positive or negative for AI safety. He concludes it has been positive, but acknowledges the concern that RLHF primarily makes AI systems more useful and commercially viable (accelerating capabilities) while providing only modest safety benefits.

His core argument: RLHF buys time by keeping humans in the loop during training, and the alternative (no human feedback, just pretraining objectives) would be worse. The real question is whether the alignment community can build on RLHF toward more robust oversight methods before capabilities advance beyond what RLHF can handle.
